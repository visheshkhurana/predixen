### 📌 Bug Report: Financial Metrics Displayed in “RunwayAI – Startup Financial Simulator” Are Incorrect

**Context**
I uploaded a PDF financial report for Arya.ag (“Arya.ag – 2025 Q2 – Mini Scan”) and used the `RunwayAI – Startup Financial Simulator` to view the company’s metrics. The tool generated metrics such as “Monthly revenue $116.1M, monthly surplus $106.0M, cash balance $2.00M, gross margin 91.3%, revenue growth 800% MoM, operating margin 91.3%.” However, the PDF shows a very different picture: Q1‑2025 revenue is $12.9 M (ARR $51.6 M), gross margin is 22.1%, operating margin is 9.9%, and YoY growth is ~1.24×. These values are off by roughly an order of magnitude.

**What’s wrong**

1. **Incorrect scaling of revenue:** The simulator displays $116 M in monthly revenue but the PDF reports only $12.9 M quarterly revenue (run rate $51.6 M).
2. **Margins are inflated:** Gross margin is shown as 91.3%, while the PDF says 22.1%. Operating margin is shown as 91.3%, but it should be about 9.9%.
3. **Growth figures are exaggerated:** The simulator reports 800% MoM growth; the PDF indicates a CQGR of 5.6% and YoY growth of ~1.24×.
4. **Unit economics mismatch:** Derived metrics like ARPU, LTV:CAC, payback period, and burn multiple appear unrealistic given the actual numbers.

**Expected behaviour**

* The simulator should parse the uploaded file and calculate metrics consistent with the source document.
* For Arya.ag, the correct metrics (per the PDF) are:

  * **Revenue (Q1 2025):** $12.9 M (ARR $51.6 M)
  * **Gross margin:** 22.1%
  * **Operating margin:** 9.9%
  * **YoY growth:** ~1.24× (not 800% MoM)
  * **Sector breakdown:** Agri‑Warehousing revenue $7.6 M / ARR $30.4 M (gross margin 11.2%, operating margin 5.2%); Financing/Commerce revenue $5.3 M / ARR $21.2 M (gross margin 36.6%, operating margin 15.6%).

**Suggested fix**

1. **Review parsing logic:** Check how the simulator extracts data from PDF reports; ensure it reads actual numeric values rather than misinterpreting units (e.g., confusing quarterly revenue for monthly and then incorrectly scaling by 9× or 12×).
2. **Validate calculations:** After extracting revenue, compute ARR properly (e.g., 4× quarterly revenue or 12× monthly revenue), and derive other metrics from these correct values.
3. **Unit consistency:** Align “M” and “B” units. For example, $51.6 M ARR should not be interpreted as $1.393 B.
4. **Sanity checks:** Include basic validations to prevent displaying impossible values like 91.3% gross margin for a company with a ~22% gross margin, or 800% MoM growth when historical data shows modest growth.
5. **Visibility of errors:** If parsing fails or values are flagged by validation rules, display a warning instead of inflated numbers.

**Steps to reproduce**

1. Upload the file “Arya.ag – 2025 Q2 – 2025‑06‑02 – Mini Scan.pdf” to the simulator.
2. Observe the metrics displayed.
3. Compare them against the values in the PDF (especially page 5 of the report).

Please investigate and fix the parsing/representation logic so that the simulator’s output accurately reflects the underlying financial data.
