Copilot Feedback Report (Predixen)
Overview
I tested the Predixen Copilot (an AI-powered financial advisor) with multiple prompts to see how it responds to common financial questions. The Copilot uses your company’s financial data and simulations to answer strategic questions.
Prompts Tested and Observations
How can I extend my runway by 6 months?
Response summary: The Copilot analysed the current runway (16.5 months) and suggested three actions: reduce burn by 15%, implement a 10 % price increase due to strong net revenue retention, and defer non critical hires. It offered to run a simulation to model the impact of those changes.
Feedback: The advice was actionable and grounded in financial metrics. It referenced monthly revenue ($45 k), gross margin (75 %), net burn ($15 k/month) and runway months. However, the interface displayed an error toast (“Failed to get response from Copilot. Using fallback mode.”) after generating the answer, which could confuse users.
What’s the riskiest assumption in my financials?
Response summary: The Copilot identified revenue concentration as the riskiest assumption. It noted that the top five customers account for 32 % of revenue and warned that losing the largest customer would reduce runway from 16.5 months to about 11 months. The Copilot recommended diversifying the customer base before the next fundraise.
Feedback: This response was insightful and provided specific metrics such as concentration of top‑five customers and runway impact. It offered clear action (increase logo count) and used data sources (Truth Scan and Benchmark) to support the conclusion. The same error toast appeared again.
Who are my top competitors and how do I differentiate?
Response summary: The Copilot did not return an answer and instead defaulted to the generic metrics prompt. An error toast indicated that it failed to get a response and used a fallback mode.
Feedback: Not handling this prompt reduces user trust. The Copilot should either provide a relevant answer (e.g., competitor analysis based on industry data) or gracefully state that competitor information isn’t available. Displaying an error without guidance leaves the user uncertain.
General Feedback
Clarity: When the Copilot responds, its suggestions are clear and use specific metrics, which is valuable for decision-making.
Actionability: The responses often include concrete next steps (e.g., reduce burn, increase prices, diversify customers). This is helpful.
Reliability: There were repeated “Failed to get response” error messages. While fallback responses were provided for some prompts, others returned no useful answer. Improving the stability of the Copilot would enhance user confidence.
User Experience: The ability to click preset prompts is convenient. However, the error toasts should be minimized or explained better. Adding a loading indicator or retry option would improve UX.
Recommendations
Improve error handling to avoid fallback failures or explain them clearly.
Expand the knowledge base so that broader strategic questions (like competitor differentiation) are answered or flagged as outside scope.
Continue to provide metric driven, actionable suggestions for financial and strategic decisions.

Date of testing: 22 January 2026 (Asia/Dubai).
