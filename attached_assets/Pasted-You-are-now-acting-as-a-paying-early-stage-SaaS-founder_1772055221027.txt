You are now acting as a **paying early-stage SaaS founder** who has just signed up for FounderConsole.ai to manage and simulate decisions for your business. Your goal is to *test this application deeply* as if you are about to use it for real strategic planning and pay a subscription.

You must evaluate the product on real founder needs, run meaningful business scenarios, track outcomes, and give honest, structured feedback.

---

## TASKS

### 1) SIGNUP & ONBOARDING EVALUATION

Start by signing up a new company profile with the following characteristics:

• Business type: SaaS B2B  
• Stage: Post-seed (choose an appropriate stage)  
• Revenue: realistic MRR values (not zeros)  
• Cash runway: realistic 12–18 months  
• Burn and headcount: realistic SaaS operating costs

Evaluate:
• Was onboarding smooth?
• Did default values help or confuse?
• How long did it take?
• Are instructions clear?
• Any dead ends, confusing labels, or missing help text?

Record step-by-step experience and rating of clarity (1–5 stars).

---

### 2) DATA CONFIDENCE & KPI TRUST TEST

After connecting real test data or sample data:

• Evaluate the Data Confidence Score.
• Inspect how dashboard KPIs (MRR, Cash, Burn, CAC, LTV, Growth) are computed.
• Check for staleness indicators and “last updated” labels.
• Evaluate whether the confidence score feels trustworthy or confusing.
• If any number looks inaccurate, explain why.

Provide at least three specific improvements for trust signals.

---

### 3) SIMULATION TESTS — REAL BUSINESS STRATEGIES

Run each of the following scenarios:

#### A) Cost Optimization
“What happens if we cut marketing spend by 25% for 6 months?”

Evaluate:
• Runway change
• Growth impact
• Cash burn impact
• Strategic clarity

---

#### B) Staff Scaling
“What happens if we hire 10 engineers and 3 sales team members next quarter?”

Evaluate:
• Burn increase
• Productivity assumptions
• Revenue impact
• Any unrealistic outputs

---

#### C) Pricing Change
“What happens if we increase prices by 15% and churn rises by 1%?”

Evaluate:
• Profit impact
• Overall business health
• Scenario clarity

---

#### D) External Shock
“What if market/VC funding declines 30% and CAC increases 20%?”

Evaluate:
• Strategy implications
• Whether the simulator gives insights or just numbers
• Whether any macro presets are available

---

For each scenario:
• Note whether results are *meaningfully different*
• Note whether p10/p50/p90 distributions diverge
• Note whether recommendations make sense
• Rate the scenario result usefulness (1–5 stars)

---

### 4) DECISION JOURNAL & TRACKING

If FoundersConsole offers a journal or history of scenarios:

• Run at least 3 scenarios
• Add retrospective notes and predictions
• Evaluate whether the tool tracks historical outcomes
• If not, describe exactly how you would want that feature to work
• Describe the UI you’d expect

---

### 5) INTEGRATIONS & REAL-TIME DATA

Test integration flows:

• Stripe (revenue)
• QuickBooks (expenses)
• HubSpot (customer/pipeline)

Evaluate:
• Ease of connection
• UI for permissions & reconnects
• Whether data appears within 5–15 minutes (not hours)
• Whether webhooks are triggered

Provide:
• Fail cases and how error states are communicated
• Suggestions for improving clarity and error recovery

---

### 6) BILLING & PRICING VALUE

Pretend you are evaluating a subscription plan:

• Evaluate pricing clarity (does product justify its price?)
• Compare to alternatives (Baremetrics, ChartMogul, Causal, Runway)
• Provide a pricing strategy recommendation

---

### 7) BUG, RELIABILITY & PERFORMANCE CHECK

While using the dashboard, observe:

• Any crashes or “something went wrong” screens
• Page load latencies
• Synchronous vs async problems
• Scenarios that take too long or hang

Document at least 5 reliability issues and severity.
